# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- Demonstrating empathy and kindness.
- Being respectful of differing opinions and experiences.
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback.
- Taking responsibility, apologizing, and learning from mistakes.
- Focusing on what is best for the community.

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- The use of sexualized language or imagery and unwelcome sexual attention.
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks.
- Public or private harassment.
- Publishing othersâ€™ private information without explicit permission.
- Other conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate.

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing standards and may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions not aligned with this Code of Conduct.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces and in public spaces when an individual is representing the project.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to project maintainers via repository contact channels.

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Maintainers may apply community impact-based consequences, including warning, temporary ban, or permanent ban, depending on severity and recurrence.
